Problem

Visual impairment is defined as the degradation of eyesight to the point where common methods will not work. Imagine being blind and hungry walking around in a city. How would you get the information to know what to eat? Most restaurants do not provide braille versions of their menus - and the usual practice is to let someone read the menu for you. What if you could get the information directly on your phone or to your smart watch? One of the barriers faced by people in the real world is accessing information such as that would affect their lifes.

Solution

With OCR (Optical Character Recognition) and AI technology it is possible to locate, and store the text of real world objects. Information about objects like menus, indoor-maps, paintings should be accessible by your phone - and you shouldnt have to look hard for it.

An app that can access information in the real world around you. Designed for everyone that wants to get information about a nearby object or a place directly on their phone.

1.

By default the application's menu will show you either a map of your surroundings or have it interpret it audibly for you. Based on the information in our database, the app will give you an oversight of locations that it supports.

2.

From there on, if you have selected a location. You will be presented with an array of information gathered from said place. A brief description of each element in the array can be given, to make the proccess more efficient. An option will be given to not read up the information automatically, for those with moderate visual impairment.

3.

Users can also submit their own pictures, if it does not exist. The picture will then be translated into text, and uploaded to the database. Different users can then view that information, as seen in the previous screenshot.

4.

The application gives you also the option display nearby locations or find a specific location, which you can find through the search engine. Users can also preview the information stored in those places.
